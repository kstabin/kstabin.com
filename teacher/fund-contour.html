Contour Drawing

Fundamentals of Art

Perry Hall High School, Baltimore MD

UNIT OBJECTIVE:

Students will complete a series of drawing exercises in order to better understand drawing from observation.

LESSON SEQUENCE:

  • Explanation of the Left verses the Right side of the Brian.
  • Pre-Instructional drawings to show the use of both sides of the brain. (ML#2)
  • Vase/Face and Monster Vase/Face (ML#3 and 4)
  • Upside down drawing (ML#5)
  • Blind Contour Drawing (ML#6)
  • Modified Contour Drawing (ML#7)
  • Point of View, Size, Placement of objects (ML#8, 9, 10)
  • Sketches of final drawing object (ML#11)
  • Thumbnail Sketches of final composition (ML#12)
  • Final composite drawing of object

GUIDING QUESTIONS:

  • What does a contour line drawing look like? The basic outline of an object, including details but not including shading.
  • How do you draw something so that it looks realistic and accurate? By observation.
  • How are we using the right side of our brain to create accurate drawings? The right side of the brain is the creative side and special oriented, we are drawing from observation and translating the space that we see in front of us into a drawing.

STATE STANDARDS / FUNDAMENTALS OF ART COURSE INDICATORS:

  • F3.1 developing skills with using a variety of sketching and drawing tools
  • F3.2 selecting and using art tools, materials, techniques and processes appropriately.
  • F4.2 listening, observing, analyzing, questioning, identifying, and comparing visual characteristics.

SKILLS AND CONCEPTS:

Declarative Knowledge:
  • Students will understand what the contour of an object looks like.
  • Students will understand that looking at an object while drawing it will give them a more detailed and accurate drawing.
Procedural Knowledge:
  • Students will be able to draw objects using just contour lines.
  • Students will be able to spend 100% of their focus looking at the subject they are drawing while not looking at their paper.

VOCABULARY:

  • Contour: A line which defines the edge of an object.
  • Edge: A part where two areas meet.
  • Blind Contour Drawing: A drawing in which you focus 100% of your attention on the subject, you do not look at your paper.
  • Right Side of the Brain: Creativity
  • Left Side of the Brian: Logic

RESOURCES AND MATERIALS:
