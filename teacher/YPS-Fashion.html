MICA YPS Summer Art Camp

"Introduction to Fashion Design"

Grades 6-8

Sept, 2011

For the fashion savvy young artist, this course introduces students to the world of style! Students explore fashion design concepts through sketching, fabric selection and design, color combinations, pattern design, and use of textures in garments. Beyond designing clothing items, students also consider fashion trends, past and present, in order to create garments, accessories, hats, and other innovative wearables that push the boundaries of existing styles. After guiding students through the basic vocabulary and skills needed to be successful fashion designers, students are presented with challenges and clients for whom they must design original creations for specific purposes.

Day 1

Designing for YOU

Students will design an outfit that best represents them; their personality, their likes, their style choices. They will design a 2d life size drawing or collage of themselves wearing this outfit.

Theme: Personal Design

Media: A variety of 2d art materials

Day 2

Figure Drawing

Students will explore a variety of figure drawing exercises addressing the shapes of the body and how clothing falls on it. They will draw from live models exploring the figure through short gesture drawings as well as extended long poses. Students will also explore adding color and details to these drawings using watercolor, creating a final fashion illustration.

Theme: Figure Drawing

Media: Charcoal, Pencil, Watercolor

Day 3

Designing for a Rock Star

Students will explore the fashion statements and personas of rock stars throughout history. They will use these exemplars to design a line of outfits for a rock star of their choosing. They will be considering personality, purpose, and culture within their designs. They will design these outfits on paper, using a variety of mediums.

Theme: Designing for a Purpose

Media: Pencil, Watercolor, Other 2d Media

Day 4

Graphic Design

Students will explore logos and other graphics that appear on clothing items such as t-shirts. They will create their own graphic that they will print on a t-shirt transfer to be placed on an actual t-shirt. So remember to bring in a T-shirt you want your design to be printed on.

Theme: Graphic Design, Logos

Media: Multimedia, Digital

Day 5

Constructing Accessories

Students will look at a variety of examples of designer accessories. They will then design an outfit inspired by nature and animals, complete with a variety of accessories. They will be given materials in which to construct a hat that can actually be worn that would go with the outfit they have designed.

Theme: Nature, Designing Accessories, Constructing Wearable Art

Media: Variety of Materials

Day 6

Fashion Photography

Using the hats students have constructed the class will hold a photo shot to capture images of their creations. They will be thinking about the environment they are photographing in, the pose and attitude of the model posing, and the message they are trying to create with their photo.

Theme: Creating a Story Through Photography

Media: Photography

Day 7

Pattern and Fiber

Students will explore how pattern is used in design and will learn how to create their own patterns. Through exploring how artists use pattern and how designers use pattern, students will create a pattern they would use for clothing. Students will also explore techniques used with and on fiber, such as basic sewing skills, embroidery, beading, silk screening, and felting.

Theme: Pattern, Fiber Techniques

Media: Variety of Materials

Day 8

Transformation

Students will explore how fashion can transform, exaggerate, and change the body. They will design an article of clothing that transforms the shape of the body using materials such as paper, cardboard, and plastic bags. They will learn construction techniques such as weaving, attaching, and bending.

Theme: Sculpture, Wearable Art, Transformation of the Figure/Body

Media: Recycled Materials, Cardboard, Other 3d Material

Day 9

ART SHOW!

Final Art Show. Students will set up their artworks in a display that will show all of their creations.
