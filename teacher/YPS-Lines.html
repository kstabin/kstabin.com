MICA YPS Summer Art Camp

"Lines, Dots, and Dashes"

Grades 1-3

June, 2012

In this class students will explore lines, dot, and dashes and other mark making techniques. Using a variety of media students will explore the qualities and effects that lines and marks express. By looking at artistic exemplars students will create paintings, drawings, portraits, prints, and sculptures. By exploring the essential elements that compose a piece of art, the marks within it, students will begin to create emotional and personal art works expressing these artistic qualities. They will do this while also exploring materials and what different mediums have to offer.

Day 1

What is a Mark?

Students will begin their art making experience by creating sketch books to keep all their planning and ideas in. We will then go on an exploration of materials and practice mark making in the styles of famous artists. Student will use their mark making knowledge to create final drawings composed of many different lines, dots, or dashes based on their emotions.

Theme: Mark Making and Materials Exploration

Media: Varied artistic medias including crayon, pastel, and marker

Day 2

Ink Blot Monsters

Students will explore the endless possibilities that a dot can offer. Through transforming a blot of ink into an imagined creature students will explore their fears and create a monster that symbolizes them.

Theme: Personal Fears

Media: Ink and Sharpie

Day 3

Pointillism Paradise

Students will continue their exploration of what can made using dots. After looking at artists such as Seurat and the pointillists, students will create their own painting of an imagined paradise using only dots as their marks.

Theme: Pointillism Painting

Media: Tempera paint

Day 4

Pointillism Paintings

Students will continue their painting using only dots. As an introduction to line students will explore what it means to be a part of a collective community, through creating a chain composed of each studentâ€™s individual drawings and statements combined together to create a single line.

Theme: Pointillism Painting and Collaborative Lines

Media: Tempera Paint

Day 5

Weaving Lines

Students will explore line through the creation of a weaving composed of a variety of materials that already exist as lines or can be formed into lines. Through combining these different lines together students will create a single composition.

Theme: Line

Media: Various materials including fabric, string, paper, plastic and branches

Day 6

Contour Drawing

Further exploring line students will learn about contour drawing, continuous- line drawing. They will create a contour drawing self-portrait and then transform this drawing into a 3d sculpture made out of wire.

Theme: Line and Contour Drawing, Self-Portraits

Media: Marker and Wire

Day 7

Nature Contours

Further exploring contour drawing, students will explore the great outdoors finding and drawing elements of nature that are composed of lines. They will draw these contours with crayon then paint there drawing with watercolor, covering the paper everywhere except where the crayon was with color, creating a watercolor resist.

Theme: Line and Contour, Nature

Media: Crayon and Watercolor

Day 8

Printmaking

Students will collect items from nature to create a collage of, which they will then print with. Students will create a drawing of their collage and further explore printmaking process by transferring this drawing onto styrofoam to also print with. Students will make a number of prints from their collagraph plate and their carved styrofoam printing plate exploring the lines of nature.

Theme: Line and Printmaking, Nature

Media: Board, Glue, Styrofoam and Printing Ink

Day 9

Printmaking

Students will finish printing with their collagraph plate as well as their styrofoam printing plate. Students will produce a number of prints using a variety of colors and papers.

Theme: Line and Printmaking, Nature

Media: Board, Glue, Styrofoam and Printing Ink

Day 10

Final Show

Student will put any finishing touches on projects and set up for the art show.
